About uMotion

About uMotion

For people with Parkinson’s disease, a Parkinsonism or similar neurological condition, mobility is no longer self-evident. A walking aid that can be adapted to individual needs, in various stages of the illness, is indispensable.

A regular walker does not take into account the changing needs, or specific symptoms of Parkinson’s, such as reduced strength in one or both hands, propulsion, tremor, muscle stiffness and freezing of gait.

The uMotion was developed especially for people with these symptoms.

The uMotion is a walking aid, based on an extremely stable u-shaped frame. Not only does the uMotion help you to walk safely, it also functions as a ‘home occupational therapist’; this is because the uMotion was developed in such a way that patients can use it while doing their therapeutic exercises.

Developed with and for each other

The uMotion was developed in close collaboration with patients, their partners, family members and care professionals. This has ensured that the aid offers all the functions necessary to securely support someone with a serious mobility disorder:

  • A reversed brake system, which prevents falls and is operable with one hand
  • A drag brake for extra control
  • Green laser light, to break freezing episodes
  • A comfortable and secure non-slip seat with back rest
  • Optimum manoeuvrability
  • A built-in kerb aid, to safely roll over kerbs and doorsteps
  • Compact and easy to store

The uMotion allows someone with Parkinson’s disease to remain mobile for longer, both outdoors and at home. The patient is then less reliant on others, and remains independent for longer.

About Villerius Medical

Villerius Medical. Our mission and vision.

We aim to create the shortest possible route between us and the users of our products. That way, we are better able to pay attention to detail, which is a continual process.  This natural way of collaborating allows latent needs to surface on their own, at which point we happily get to work on them.

Independently mobile. That is something we feel everyone deserves. And that is how we arrived at the uMotion.

Together with patients, their partners and care professionals, Villerius Medical has successfully developed a walking aid which helps people with Parkinson’s disease, a Parkinsonism, or a similar neurological condition to stay independent for longer.

The uMotion as a therapeutic walking aid

If you are suffering from Parkinson's disease, movement is good for you, as a way of keeping you fit for as long as possible. A physiotherapist or occupational therapist can help you with that. However, a weekly trip to the practice can be challenging. Using the uMotion, you can simply do your exercises in your own familiar environment, in the comfort of your own home. The uMotion's unique stability and reliable braking system make every exercise safe and effective.

You can look at the exercises online and switch them on when you wish to start exercising. You can also look at the exercises in the brochure. We recommend you respect your body’s limitations and don't ignore them. Perform the exercises the first time together with, and supervised by, your therapist.

Reversed breaking system

The uMotion is on the brake as standard, so it won't roll away from you. The uMotion will only roll if you squeeze one or both of the brake handles. As soon as you let go of them again, the uMotion immediately stops. The braking system can also be operated with just one hand.
All properties

Drag brake

For extra control, the drag brake on the uMotion can be adjusted to the resistance desired. The drag brake can also be adjusted asymmetrically.
All properties

Green laser light

A well-known symptom of Parkinson's disease is freezing of gait (FOG). The uMotion is equipped with a laser beam that projects a green line between the rear wheels. Stepping across the green line breaks the freezing episode.

Advantages of the
uMotion for
care professionals.

Using the uMotion, you can offer
your clients better help and support,
and maintain the care plan.

Care professionals, such as physiotherapists and occupational therapists, play an important part in the lives of people with Parkinson's disease. No-one knows better than they how to help and support each patient in their own specific phase of the disease. Read here about their clients and what uMotion means for them.

The reason uMotion is particularly useful to care professionals is that, in addition to being a walking aid, it is also a therapeutic exercise aid. The care professional can arrange with the patient which exercises can be done at home, using the uMotion. These exercises can be done independently, with the help of instruction films.
