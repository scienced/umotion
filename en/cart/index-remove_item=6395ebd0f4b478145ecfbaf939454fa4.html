About uMotion

About uMotion

For people with Parkinson’s disease, a Parkinsonism or similar neurological condition, mobility is no longer self-evident. A walking aid that can be adapted to individual needs, in various stages of the illness, is indispensable.

A regular walker does not take into account the changing needs, or specific symptoms of Parkinson’s, such as reduced strength in one or both hands, propulsion, tremor, muscle stiffness and freezing of gait.

The uMotion was developed especially for people with these symptoms.

The uMotion is a walking aid, based on an extremely stable u-shaped frame. Not only does the uMotion help you to walk safely, it also functions as a ‘home occupational therapist’; this is because the uMotion was developed in such a way that patients can use it while doing their therapeutic exercises.

Developed with and for each other

The uMotion was developed in close collaboration with patients, their partners, family members and care professionals. This has ensured that the aid offers all the functions necessary to securely support someone with a serious mobility disorder:

  • A reversed brake system, which prevents falls and is operable with one hand
  • A drag brake for extra control
  • Green laser light, to break freezing episodes
  • A comfortable and secure non-slip seat with back rest
  • Optimum manoeuvrability
  • A built-in kerb aid, to safely roll over kerbs and doorsteps
  • Compact and easy to store

The uMotion allows someone with Parkinson’s disease to remain mobile for longer, both outdoors and at home. The patient is then less reliant on others, and remains independent for longer.

About Villerius Medical

Villerius Medical. Our mission and vision.

We want to help in the struggle to offer people with Parkinson’s disease the best quality of life possible. We do this by way of a well thought out walking aid produced in own management. A second product is currently in the ‘proof of concept’ phase and the patent is pending.

We prefer, where possible, to work only with people who add value to our efforts to better serve our target group, and try to avoid ‘interference on the line’, as that is counter-productive.

We aim to create the shortest possible route between us and the users of our products. That way, we are better able to pay attention to detail, which is a continual process.  This natural way of collaborating allows latent needs to surface on their own, at which point we happily get to work on them.

Independently mobile. That is something we feel everyone deserves. And that is how we arrived at the uMotion.

Together with patients, their partners and care professionals, Villerius Medical has successfully developed a walking aid which helps people with Parkinson’s disease, a Parkinsonism, or a similar neurological condition to stay independent for longer.
